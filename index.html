About

CATV (originally "community antenna television," now often "community access television") is more commonly known as "cable TV." Cable television began in the United States as a commercial business in 1950s. Cable Television works by connecting subscriber residences to the cable company's local distribution facility through coaxial cables using towers and utility poles. In most cases the signal travels through distribution hubs and nodes specific for a certain town or region before ending up in the homes of subscribers. The signal coming from the coaxial cable is then transferred to cable boxes and translated to the connected television. Many channels can be transmitted through one coaxial cable by a technique called frequency division multiplexing in which each channel receives a different frequency, allowing the subscriber to switch between channels. The cable boxes are also encrypted and allow only frequencies of the channels subscribed to get through to the end user.
